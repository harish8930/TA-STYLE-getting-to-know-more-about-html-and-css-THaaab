The beautiful state

Himachal Pradesh is a northern Indian state in the Himalayas. It's home to scenic mountain towns and resorts such as Dalhousie. Host to the Dalai Lama, Himachal Pradesh has a strong Tibetan presence. This is reflected in its Buddhist temples and monasteries, as well as its vibrant Tibetan New Year celebrations. The region is also well known for its trekking, climbing and skiing areas.

Himachal Pradesh, state of India, in the extreme northern part of the Asian subcontinent. It is bounded by Jammu and Kashmir union territory to the northwest and Ladakh union territory to the northeast, by the Tibet Autonomous Region of China to the east, and by the states of Uttarakhand to the southeast, Haryana to the south, and Punjab to the west. Himachal Pradesh occupies a region of scenic splendour in the western Himalayas, offering a multitextured display of lofty snow-clad mountains, deep gorges, thickly forested valleys, large lakes, terraced fields, and cascading streams. Indeed, the name of the state is a reference to its setting: Himachal means “snowy slopes” (Sanskrit: hima, “snow”; acal, “slopes”), and Pradesh means “state.”

Hillsation of Himachal Pradesh

Himachal Pradesh is probably one of the most enchanting hill states in India. Set amidst the western Himalayas, the state is blessed with majestic snow-clad mountains, gushing rivers, beautiful meadows, quaint villages and magical hill stations, which are an epitome of beauty. These hill stations in Himachal are a haven for honeymooners, adventure seekers and wildlife enthusiasts

List of Hillstation

Spiti Spiti is a heaven for solitude seekers and adventure lovers. The place is so enchantingly beautiful and secluded that it attracts thousands of travellers every year. A majestic cold desert valley in Himachal Pradesh, Spiti is home to some incredible and old monasteries and untouched villages that will leave you awe-stuck!

Shimla The capital of Himachal, Shimla is one of the most remarkable hill stations in India, with a unique old-world charm. The colonial hill station is some 342 km from Delhi, and makes for a great weekend getaway. The place turns into a magical land, when it snows and the beauty just multiplies!

Manali The list won’t be complete without mentioning Manali. A piece of heaven on earth, Manali is every bit beautiful and magical. Nestled in the mighty Himalayas, this historic hill station has valleys that are dotted with oak, fir, deodar and pine forests, adding to the romantic side of the place. The place also scores high when it comes to historic temples. Devotees visit Manali to pray at the temples such as the Hadimba Devi Temple and Manu Temple.

Kufri This magical hill town is only a few kilometres from Shimla and scores high on scenic beauty. The place is absolutely popular among newlyweds as it has a unique romantic charm. One of the most sought-after summer destinations in India, Kufri impresses with its mighty snow-covered mountains and pretty resorts.

Dharamshala You’ll be stunned with the enthralling natural beauty of Dharamshala! The beautiful hill station is all about Buddhist monasteries, gorgeous waterfalls, and tiny little cafes perched on hill tops. Set in the backdrop of the Dhauladhar ranges, Dharamshala is one of the star attractions of Himachal Pradesh and attracts travellers and adventure enthusiasts from all over the world.

Chamba There are songs written and sung about the beauty of Chamba! The pretty little hill station makes for an ideal retreat for people seeking spirituality and solitude amid untouched nature. The place has many sacred shrines and temples, belonging to the 4th century! One of the most famous temples here is the Laxmi Narayan Temple, featuring exquisite architecture.

Himachal Pradesh tourism has something in store for nature lovers. Its beauty lies in its diversity. You have lush valleys like Kinnaur, Kullu and Kangra & barren terrains of Lahaul & Spiti. Tales of Malana fascinate tourists as much as the Buddhist culture in Mcleodganj. Exploring Dalhousie, with its rich colonial past, is sheer delight. The famous Hill stations like Shimla, Kullu & Manali are dotted with meadows, orchards, quaint villages & rich colonial past. Any sightseeing tour of Himachal should include its wonderful lakes. Quiet and still, they reflect the surroundings, both literally and symbolically. A few of the famous lakes are Renuka, Rewalsar, Prashar, Suraj Tal & Chandratal. The views of the Himalayan Ranges fill you with excitement, from head to toe.
